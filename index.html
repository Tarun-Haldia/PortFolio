// Machine vs Human

Why Machines Win at Hard Problems

Try these three challenges yourself — then watch the algorithm solve them instantly. This is exactly why ML matters.

THE CHALLENGE

Travelling Salesman Problem

Connect all cities with the shortest possible route — visiting each exactly once and returning home. With 12 cities there are 239 million possible routes. Humans take minutes and rarely find the optimal. A greedy algorithm + 2-opt improvement solves it in milliseconds.

📐 Why it's hard for humans

Your brain can only hold ~7 items in working memory at once. The optimal path requires comparing all route combinations simultaneously — something biological brains cannot do, but algorithms handle trivially.

THE CHALLENGE

Number Pattern Recognition

Look at a sequence and predict the next 3 numbers. Sounds simple — until the patterns nest, combine, or hide. A trained regression model finds the formula in the data without being told what it is.

🔢 Why it's hard for humans

Humans look for familiar patterns (fibonacci, squares, primes). But real-world data rarely fits one clean rule. ML models fit any underlying function — including combinations humans would never guess.

Guess the next number, then see what the AI finds.

Maze Solver — BFS vs Human

Navigate this maze from S to E. Use arrow keys or click adjacent cells. Then watch Breadth-First Search find the guaranteed shortest path — exploring every possibility at once like a wave, not one path at a time.

🌀 Why it's hard for humans

Humans explore one path until hitting a dead end, then backtrack. BFS explores all paths simultaneously in layers. It cannot miss the shortest path — it's mathematically guaranteed to find it. Humans rarely do on first try.
